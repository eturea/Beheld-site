What the companion writes

Statements. Not prompts, not questions, not goals.

It does not coach. It does not score. It does not say "walked" or "ran" or "climbed." It uses words like covered, went, gained, returned. Distance and landmarks lead.

On an ordinary day

"The length of Central Park. Two kilometers, folded into yesterday."

On rest

"About a kilometer went into yesterday. Rest stayed part of the record."

On a gain

"Kilimanjaro's height, gained yesterday. The rise was there before anyone thought to count it."

On returning

"Crater Lake, matched in pieces. Six kilometers, recorded without ceremony."

Postcards

Some days arrive as keepsakes.

When a milestone appears in the record, Beheld turns the distance into an illustrated postcard and leaves it in the journal. On eligible iPhones, paid Personalization users receive postcards generated on the device for the milestone at hand. Otherwise, Beheld quietly uses a bundled illustration.

Generated images stay on your device.

Worth keeping. Worth sending.

Private by design

Designed to work on your iPhone, and nowhere else.

Beheld reads Apple Health on the iPhone and writes there. Your movement history is not uploaded to Beheld, an ad network, or a server.

Writing runs on device, where Apple Intelligence is available. Postcard images are generated on device, where Image Playground is available.

There is no account. No external analytics. Purchases are handled by Apple through the App Store; Beheld does not receive payment details.

Free, then personal

One free companion. One small purchase.

The free version keeps writing, every morning — no subscription, no time limit. A one-time $4.99 purchase unlocks Personalization, and the companion begins to write about your own movement in more specific ways.
